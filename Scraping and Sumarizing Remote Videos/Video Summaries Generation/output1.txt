Overview:
The narrative revolves around the presumed dead Henry Armstrong, who awakens in his grave during a grave robbery, leading to a series of events marked by fear and chaos among the grave robbers.

Key Takeaways:
1. Henry Armstrong is buried alive but, due to his indifference caused by illness, remains unaware of his dire situation until grave robbers unwittingly awaken him.
2. The grave robbers, two medical students and a cemetery worker named Jess, are terrified when Armstrong sits up, prompting them to flee the scene.
3. The story concludes with Jess bringing Armstrong's body to a medical college, awaiting payment, as Armstrong's true fate is revealed by his lifeless form due to a fatal head injury inflicted during the event.

Important Details:
- The setting is a dark, stormy night in a cemetery, enhancing the eerie atmosphere.
- Jess, unlike the students, does not flee far but seems resigned and business-like in his demeanor at the end. 
- Armstrong's initial 'awakening' and subsequent death underscore themes of misperception and indifference to one's own fate.
